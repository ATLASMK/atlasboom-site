Boom Pumps vs. Line Pumps: Which Do You Need?

The right pump depends on access, volume, and pour height:

  • Boom pumps use a truck-mounted robotic arm to place concrete over obstacles and up to high floors. Best for large-volume pours, high-rise work, and sites where the truck can stay on the street.
  • Line pumps push concrete through ground-level hoses to reach tight, low-clearance, or hard-to-access areas. Best for slabs, footings, pool decks, basements, and smaller residential pours.

If you are not sure which fits your jobsite, tell us the pour location, volume in cubic yards, and access, and we will recommend the right unit.

Why Concrete Pumping Beats Chutes and Wheelbarrows

On Chicago jobsites where space is tight and schedules are tighter, pumping concrete saves labor and reduces cold joints by placing concrete faster and more consistently. A pump can reach across landscaping, over a finished structure, or into a deep excavation without tearing up the site — work that would take a crew hours by hand.

Chicago Weather and Pour Scheduling

Illinois winters demand careful planning. Cold-weather concreting requires attention to mix temperature, placement speed, and protection after the pour. A reliable pump and operator keep placement quick so the concrete is finished and protected before the cold works against it. In summer, fast placement helps you beat flash setting on hot, windy days. We schedule around your concrete delivery so the pump is ready when the trucks arrive.

What Affects Concrete Pumping Cost?

Pricing depends on pump type (line vs. boom), pour volume, reach and setup, and on-site time. Boom pumps carry a higher base rate than line pumps because of their reach and capacity. The best way to budget is a quick quote based on your pour size and access.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you provide the pump operator?

Yes. Every pump comes with a trained operator who runs the machine and manages the placement.

How much concrete can a boom pump place per hour?

Output varies by mix and setup, but boom pumps move high volumes quickly — far faster than placing by chute or wheelbarrow.

Can you reach a backyard or interior pour?

Yes. Line pumps run hose through gates, doorways, and tight spaces; boom pumps reach over structures from the street.

How far in advance should I book?

Book as early as you can lock your concrete delivery date, especially during the busy spring and summer season.
