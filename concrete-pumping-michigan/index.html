Boom Pump vs. Line Pump

  • Boom pumps reach over and up with a truck-mounted arm — best for large pours, tilt-up panels, and multi-story placement.
  • Line pumps run hose along the ground into tight or enclosed spaces — best for footings, residential slabs, basements, and interior work.

Tell us the pour volume, location, and access, and we will match the right pump to the job.

Pouring Through Michigan Winters

Few states test a concrete schedule like Michigan. Long, hard freezes mean cold-weather concreting is the rule for much of the year, and getting concrete placed and protected quickly is critical to strength and durability. A pump moves concrete fast and consistently so your crew can finish and cover the pour before the cold compromises it. In summer, that same speed helps beat quick setting on hot days. We coordinate timing with your ready-mix supplier so trucks and pump arrive together.

Why Pump Instead of Pour by Hand?

On busy Michigan jobsites, pumping reduces labor, limits cold joints, and reaches places a truck cannot — across landscaping, over a structure, or into a deep foundation. The result is a cleaner pour completed in less time.

What Affects Pumping Cost?

Pump type, pour volume, reach and setup, and on-site time all factor in, with boom pumps priced above line pumps for their reach and capacity. A quick quote based on your pour details gives the most accurate budget.
