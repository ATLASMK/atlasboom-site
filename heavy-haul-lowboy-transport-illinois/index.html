Heavy Haul & Lowboy Transport in Illinois

Moving an excavator, crane, transformer, or modular unit across the state takes more than a trailer — it takes the right deck, the right permits, and a driver who knows the routes. ATLAS Chicago LLC provides heavy haul and lowboy transport in Illinois, including permitted oversize and overweight moves in and out of the Chicago metro and across the I-55, I-80, I-88, and I-90 corridors.

Trailers We Run

The deck makes the move. We match your machine to the correct trailer:

  • Lowboy (RGN) trailers — low deck height for tall equipment like excavators and dozers; the gooseneck detaches so machines drive on
  • Step deck and flat deck — for legal-height freight that loads from the rear
  • Multi-axle and extendable lowboys — for very heavy or long loads that need extra axles or length (stretching roughly 28 ft up to 50 ft)

What We Haul

Excavators, bulldozers, wheel loaders, motor graders, cranes, compactors, transformers, generators, modular structures, and other oversize industrial equipment.

Illinois Oversize & Overweight Permit Basics

In Illinois, a load is legal when it stays within standard limits — generally up to 8 ft 6 in wide, 13 ft 6 in high, and 80,000 lb gross. Anything beyond that needs an oversize/overweight permit from the Illinois Department of Transportation (IDOT) (and separate permits for the Illinois Tollway). Key points to plan around:

  • Oversize moves generally travel daylight hours, Monday through Saturday (until noon) — no Sunday or holiday travel for oversize loads
  • The Chicago metro area often restricts oversize moves during morning and afternoon rush hours
  • Pilot/escort vehicles are typically required as loads get wider (commonly above ~14 ft), with additional escorts for extreme widths
  • Routing must avoid low clearances and weight-restricted bridges

Rules change, so we confirm current requirements with IDOT for every permitted move.

Pilot Cars and Route Planning

We arrange certified pilot cars, plan compliant routes around Chicago's tolls, low bridges, and traffic windows, and handle the permit paperwork so your equipment arrives legally and on time. For super loads, we coordinate route surveys and any required police escorts.

Why Local Knowledge Matters in Illinois

Hauling through Chicagoland is not like running open interstate. Toll structures, height-restricted viaducts, congested interchanges, and tight delivery sites all shape the route. Choosing a hauler who runs these lanes every week keeps your move out of trouble and on schedule.

Service Area and Lanes

Statewide Illinois coverage with frequent lanes between Chicago, Rockford, Joliet, Peoria, Springfield, and the Quad Cities, plus interstate moves to neighboring Indiana, Wisconsin, Michigan, Ohio, and Pennsylvania.

What Affects Heavy Haul Cost?

Heavy haul pricing reflects distance, weight, dimensions, trailer type, permits, and escorts. As a general industry guide, legal loads run roughly $3.50–$4.50 per mile, while permitted oversize loads run higher, and each state crossed adds its own permit cost. We quote the full move — transport, permits, and escorts — up front.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a lowboy and an RGN trailer?

An RGN (removable gooseneck) is a type of lowboy whose front detaches into a ramp so equipment can drive on. Both offer a low deck for tall loads.

Do you handle the oversize permits?

Yes. We pull IDOT and tollway permits, plan the route, and arrange any required pilot cars.

Can you move a crane or excavator out of state?

Yes. We run interstate heavy haul from Illinois to Indiana, Michigan, Wisconsin, Ohio, Pennsylvania, and beyond.

How far ahead should I schedule an oversize move?

Permitted moves need lead time for permits and escorts — contact us as early as possible to hold your date.
