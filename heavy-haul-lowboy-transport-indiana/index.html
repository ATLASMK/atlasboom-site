Heavy Haul & Lowboy Transport in Indiana

Indiana sits at the center of the nation's freight network, where I-65, I-69, I-70, I-74, I-80/90, and I-94 all converge. That makes it a natural lane for heavy haul and lowboy transport in Indiana — and a state where knowing the routes and the permit rules pays off. ATLAS Chicago LLC moves construction and industrial equipment across Indiana and to neighboring states with the right trailers, permits, and escorts.

Trailers for Every Load

  • Lowboy (RGN) trailers — low deck for tall machines like excavators, dozers, and cranes; the gooseneck removes so equipment drives on
  • Step deck and flat deck — for legal-height freight loaded from the rear
  • Multi-axle and extendable lowboys — for extra-heavy or extra-long loads needing more axles or deck length

What We Move

Excavators, dozers, loaders, graders, cranes, compactors, transformers, generators, agricultural and industrial machinery, and modular components.

Indiana Oversize & Overweight Permit Basics

A load is legal in Indiana within standard limits — generally 8 ft 6 in wide, 13 ft 6 in high, and 80,000 lb gross. Beyond that, you need an oversize/overweight permit from the Indiana Department of Transportation (INDOT). A few things that make Indiana relatively haul-friendly:

  • INDOT single-trip permits offer a generous validity window, giving real flexibility to schedule a move
  • Oversize travel is generally allowed sunrise to sunset, Monday through Saturday, with Sunday by exception
  • Very heavy loads (in the six-figure pound range) can trigger a bridge analysis and a special routing review
  • Pilot/escort vehicles are required as widths increase, with routing planned around low clearances

Because rules and thresholds change, we confirm current requirements with INDOT for each permitted move.

Permits, Escorts, and Routing Handled for You

We pull the INDOT permits, plan a compliant route around restricted bridges and clearances, and arrange certified pilot cars where the load requires them. For super loads, we coordinate route surveys and any required police escorts so the move stays legal end to end.

Service Area and Lanes

What Affects Heavy Haul Cost?

Distance, weight, dimensions, trailer type, permits, and escorts all factor in. As a general guide, legal loads run roughly $3.50–$4.50 per mile, permitted oversize loads run higher, and each state on the route adds a permit cost.
