Michigan Oversize & Overweight Permit Basics

In Michigan, loads within standard limits — generally 8 ft 6 in wide, 13 ft 6 in high, and 80,000 lb gross — travel legal. Beyond those limits you need an oversize/overweight permit from the Michigan Department of Transportation (MDOT). Plan around these points:

  • Oversize moves generally travel daylight hours, Monday through Saturday
  • Pilot/escort vehicles are commonly required once a load exceeds roughly 12 ft wide, with more escorts for greater widths
  • Michigan's axle and weight rules can allow heavier configurations with additional axles, but routing and bridge limits still govern the move
  • Routes must avoid low clearances and weight-restricted bridges

Requirements change, so we confirm current MDOT rules for each permitted load.

What Affects Heavy Haul Cost?

Distance, weight, dimensions, trailer type, permits, and escorts all play in. As a general guide, legal loads run roughly $3.50–$4.50 per mile, permitted oversize loads run higher, and each state crossed adds its own permit fees. We quote the full move — transport, permits, and escorts — up front.
