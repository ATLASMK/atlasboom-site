Ohio Oversize & Overweight Permit Basics

Loads within standard limits — generally 8 ft 6 in wide, 13 ft 6 in high, and 80,000 lb gross — travel legal in Ohio. Beyond that, you need an oversize/overweight permit from the Ohio Department of Transportation (ODOT). Plan around these escort thresholds, which are typical for Ohio:

  • One pilot/escort vehicle is generally required for loads over about 13 ft wide on two-lane roads
  • Two escorts are commonly required over about 14 ft 6 in wide
  • A police escort may be required for loads exceeding roughly 16 ft wide
  • Oversize travel is generally daylight-only, with weekend and holiday restrictions

The Ohio Turnpike issues its own permits, and rules change, so we confirm current ODOT and Turnpike requirements for each move.

Permits, Escorts, and Routing Handled for You

We pull ODOT (and Turnpike) permits, plan a compliant route around restricted bridges and clearances, and arrange certified pilot cars or police escorts as the load requires. For super loads, we coordinate route surveys so the move stays legal from origin to destination.

What Affects Heavy Haul Cost?

Distance, weight, dimensions, trailer type, permits, and escorts all factor in. As a general guide, legal loads run roughly $3.50–$4.50 per mile, permitted oversize loads run higher, and each state crossed adds its own permit cost.
