Pennsylvania Oversize & Overweight Permit Basics

Loads within standard limits — generally 8 ft 6 in wide, 13 ft 6 in high, and 80,000 lb gross — travel legal in Pennsylvania. Beyond that, you need an oversize/overweight permit from the Pennsylvania Department of Transportation (PennDOT), and the Pennsylvania Turnpike issues its own separate permits. Plan around these points:

  • Oversize travel is generally daylight-only, with weekend and holiday restrictions
  • Pilot/escort vehicles are required as loads get wider, with additional escorts for extreme widths
  • Pennsylvania's mountainous terrain, tunnels, and older bridges make clearance, grade, and weight routing especially important
  • Routes must avoid low clearances and posted weight-restricted structures

Because rules change, we confirm current PennDOT and Turnpike requirements for each permitted move.

Permits, Escorts, and Routing Handled for You

What Affects Heavy Haul Cost?

Distance, weight, dimensions, trailer type, permits, and escorts all factor in. As a general guide, legal loads run roughly $3.50–$4.50 per mile, permitted oversize loads run higher, and each state crossed adds its own permit cost.
